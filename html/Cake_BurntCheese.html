BURNT CHEESE CAKE

RM10
FLOUR : 5/10
SUGAR : 7/10
TASTE : 8/10

Burnt cheesecake is a type of cheesecake that is characterized by its rustic and slightly burnt appearance. This dessert originated in the Basque Country of Spain and has since become popular all around the world, with many people falling in love with its unique taste and texture.

What sets burnt cheesecake apart from other types of cheesecake is its baking method. Instead of baking it in a water bath like traditional cheesecake, burnt cheesecake is baked at a high temperature, usually around 450°F, which causes the top to caramelize and turn a beautiful golden brown color. This gives the cheesecake its distinctive burnt appearance, which can be quite stunning.

One of the things that makes burnt cheesecake so popular is its texture. Unlike traditional cheesecake, which is dense and creamy, burnt cheesecake is light and airy, with a slightly custardy texture. The burnt top adds a crispy crunch to each bite, while the creamy, smooth center melts in your mouth.

The flavor of burnt cheesecake is also unique. While it still has the creamy, tangy taste of regular cheesecake, the caramelized top gives it a slightly sweet, toasty flavor that is absolutely delicious. Some people even describe the taste as being similar to crème brûlée, with its sweet, caramelized topping and creamy center.

One of the great things about burnt cheesecake is its versatility. While it can be served on its own as a dessert, it can also be topped with fresh fruit, whipped cream, or a drizzle of caramel sauce for added flavor. Some people even use it as a base for other desserts, like a cheesecake parfait or a cheesecake trifle.

Overall, burnt cheesecake is a unique and delicious dessert that is well worth trying. Its slightly burnt appearance may seem unusual at first, but once you take a bite, you'll understand why so many people have fallen in love with this amazing dessert.
